Astrophysics and Cosmology In A Nutshell

Research Interests

  • Supernovae: I have a profound interest in the study of transient astrophysical events, particularly supernovae. My work includes the observation, analysis, and modeling of supernovae, as well as the investigation of their connections with phenomena like gamma-ray bursts.

  • Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs): I focus on understanding the properties of tidal disruption events, their light curve behaviors, and the development of accurate mass estimators for supermassive black holes based on these events.

  • Gravitational Effects and Black Holes: My research extends to exploring the intriguing realm of gravitational effects, including Lense-Thirring precession, and their implications for our understanding of the universe.

My work is centered around data-driven approaches in astrophysics and cosmology, involving extensive data analysis and the application of advanced mathematical methods AI tools to address complex questions in the field.

Teaching and Supervision

I have the privilege of instructing introductory courses in various fields, including physics, cosmology, general relativity, and data analysis in scientific research. These courses are conducted in both English and Chinese, enabling me to connect with a diverse group of students.

As a passionate educator, I take great care in designing course agendas that foster a deeper understanding of these complex subjects. My goal is to make challenging concepts accessible and engaging for all learners.

In addition to teaching, I actively supervise and mentor students in their research projects. It is immensely rewarding to guide and support the next generation of researchers as they explore the frontiers of knowledge in the field.
