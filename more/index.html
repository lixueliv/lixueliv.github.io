I am also a volunteer with the Red Cross and Dalian Charity, where I help with tasks such as picking up trash, training newcomers, and supporting people, especially school students and the elderly. These experiences are one of the motivations that drive me to work harder!

Yes, I do Taiji. Suprise? My Taiji journey started with a book given to my father by an elderly gentleman he once helped. The book, titled something like ‘The Eight Brocades’, promised good health and vitality. Fast forward to college, where I joined in on Taiji as part of a quirky tradition – not for competitions, but for health and relaxation.

Cooking has always been a cherished part of my life. As a child, my mom’s salad was the highlight of our Chinese New Year feasts, disappearing in no time. My father excels at cooking vegetables, turning simple ingredients into nutritious delights. Though my busy schedule limits my cooking time, every opportunity in the kitchen is a fun adventure. Maybe one day, I’ll perfect a dish that wows my family! 🍽
